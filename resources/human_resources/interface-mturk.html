Survey Guidelines: Introduction

The goal of this study is to determine whether a given statement can be inferred or entailed from a provided paragraph.

Instructions:

You will be given a paragraph followed by a statement (a piece of information). Your task is to carefully read both and decide whether the statement is supported by the paragraph. You must select one of the following options:

  • ✅ Yes: The statement can be inferred or entailed from the paragraph.
  • ❌ No: The statement cannot be inferred or entailed from the paragraph.

When to Say Yes or No

Say "Yes" when:
  • The statement is directly stated in the paragraph.
  • The statement is strongly implied by the paragraph, meaning that even though the exact wording is not used, it logically follows from the information provided.
Say "No" when:
  • The statement introduces new information that is not mentioned in the paragraph.
  • The statement is about a different subject than paragraph.
  • The statement contradicts something stated in the paragraph.
  • The statement requires additional assumptions beyond what is explicitly or logically implied.

Entailment Cases (Yes)

✅ Example 1: Directly Stated

  • Paragraph: "Emily loves reading books. She spends at least an hour every night reading before bed."
  • Statement: "Emily spends an hour reading every night."
  • ✔ Answer: Yes (The statement is directly stated in the paragraph.)

✅ Example 2: Strongly Implied

  • Paragraph: "After running five miles, Tom was drenched in sweat and breathing heavily. He immediately grabbed a bottle of water."
  • Statement: "Tom was tired after his run."
  • ✔ Answer: Yes (Although the paragraph does not say "Tom was tired," his heavy breathing and need for water strongly imply it.)

Non-Entailment Cases (No)

❌ Example 3: New Information Introduced

  • Paragraph: "The sun was shining, and children were playing in the park. A group of friends sat on a bench, enjoying their ice cream."
  • Statement: "The children were playing soccer."
  • ✘ Answer: No (The paragraph mentions children playing but does not specify that they were playing soccer.)

❌ Example 4: Contradiction

  • Paragraph: "Alice was excited to go to the beach, but when she arrived, it started raining heavily. She had to stay inside."
  • Statement: "Alice enjoyed a sunny day at the beach."
  • ✘ Answer: No (The paragraph states that it was raining, contradicting the statement.)

❌ Example 5: Requires Additional Assumptions

  • Paragraph: "Mark set his alarm for 6 AM and went to bed early."
  • Statement: "Mark woke up at 6 AM."
  • ✘ Answer: No (The paragraph states he set the alarm but does not confirm he actually woke up at that time.)

❌ Example 6: Different Subject

  • Paragraph: "There are around 1,400 species of bamboo which are all considered safe and non-toxic for your pet cats."
  • Statement: "Thankfully, spider plants are not toxic to cats and so are a great option for pet owners."
  • ✘ Answer: No (However both the statement and paragraph are talking about plants that are safe for cat, but one is talking about bamboo and the other one is talking about spider plant.)
